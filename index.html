Recognizing the Signs, Reclaiming Control

Learn how to spot symptoms and take mindful steps toward healing.

Anxiety Overload

Symptoms: Racing thoughts, tense muscles, restlessness, excessive worry about everyday situations.

Support Strategies:
  • Practice deep breathing or grounding exercises.
  • Limit caffeine and get regular sleep.
  • Seek therapy — CBT is particularly effective.
  • Use mindfulness apps to track triggers and self-regulate.

Depressive Spiral

Symptoms: Persistent sadness, loss of interest, fatigue, withdrawal from others, difficulty concentrating.

Support Strategies:
  • Establish small, achievable daily routines.
  • Connect with supportive friends or family — even a quick check-in helps.
  • Consider professional support (therapy + medication options).
  • Try light exposure therapy or gentle movement like yoga.
Visit site

Burnout & Fatigue

Symptoms: Feeling drained, detached, irritable, hopeless, or chronically overwhelmed by responsibilities.

Support Strategies:
  • Create space for rest — mental breaks count as healing time.
  • Set realistic boundaries and say no without guilt.
  • Engage in restorative activities (nature walks, journaling, creative outlets).
  • Talk with a counselor to rebuild emotional resilience.

Informative Advice

Talk It Through

Sharing your thoughts with someone you trust can lighten the weight. You don’t need to have the “right” words — just starting the conversation helps. If you're not ready to talk to someone you know, Samaritans (116 123, 24/7) and Shout (text ‘SHOUT’ to 85258) offer confidential, judgment-free spaces to be heard.

Visit site

Breathe Into Calm

Take a moment to pause. Deep breathing activates your parasympathetic nervous system and soothes your stress response. Try the 4-7-8 method — inhale for 4 seconds, hold for 7, exhale for 8. Apps like Headspace or Calm offer guided breathing and mindfulness sessions. Many NHS mental health services also offer free wellbeing tools online.

Visit site

You're Not Alone

Feeling overwhelmed isn’t a failure — it’s a signal. Reach for support as you would extend a hand to someone else. Compassion starts with you. The NHS offers a confidential mental health support line, and charities like Rethink Mental Illness or YoungMinds have online support tailored to different ages and needs.

Visit site

Nourish Your Mind

What we consume mentally shapes how we feel. Gently curate your environment — reduce doomscrolling, follow accounts that uplift, and notice what gives you energy. Mind offers articles, worksheets, and free self-care tips designed to gently guide your habits toward healing. Check out their “How to improve your mental wellbeing” section.

Visit site

Move With Kindness

Physical movement isn’t about intensity — it’s about presence. Even gentle stretching or walking outdoors can lift your mood, especially when paired with soft music or natural surroundings. Local community centres, leisure groups, and even online yoga sessions can offer movement in safe, supported spaces. The Mental Health Foundation offers free movement-based mental wellbeing resources.
